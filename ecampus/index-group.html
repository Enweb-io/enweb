Importance Of Group Discussions
  • By Campus News

Group discussion allow student to exchange and share ideas and knowledge which is used to collectively learn and find solution to complex tasks and problems. Group discussion is the most effective study technique in the history of higher education. Discussion is important to learning in all disciplines because it helps students process and retain information rather than simply receive it. Leading a discussion requires skills different from lecturing. The goal of a discussion is to get students to practice thinking about the course material and task as hand such as assignments or upcoming tests presentations and exams.

It improves your thinking, listening and speaking skills. It also promotes your confidence level. It is an effective tool in problem solving, decision making and academic assessment. GD skills may ensure academic success, satisfactory CAs, improved grades and better presentations.

To start planning a discussion decide what your group want to get out of the discussion. For example, do you want them to revise an old exam or test paper, is it a group assignment, do you want make new corrections, and articulate the implications of reaching your objective? Should they be able to work certain problems by the end of the hour or discussion? Should they be able to interpret and critique a journalistic photograph or a piece of art? Deciding on and articulating the objective for the discussion will help you decide what kinds of discussion activities will best help your group reach that objective.

Remember that you can organize a discussion in many different ways: it is best to have students work in small groups. A recommended maximum is 10 and minimum of 5. If the group is too large it will take a lot of time to reach your objective as you have to listen to each and every member of the group. Small groups makes it easy to delegate roles. You will also want to leave time to wrap up and summarize the discussion.

Emphasis is placed on commitment as each member of the group is required to bring something to the table it is a give and take arrangement. This forces you to research and understand the subject better and return information in the long run, sharing information via a discussion after a research helps you commit data to memory.

"You’ve got to get up every morning with determination if you’re going to go to bed with satisfaction."

knowing the content to be covered is not enough. Naming the chapter your students will read is not enough. If you’ve only thought as far as, “I want students to know ...” you haven't thought through enough what needs to be accomplished. You should be able to articulate what the students will be able to do with the information or ideas. For example, in a philosophy class for which students have read a chapter on epistemologies or theories of knowledge, you might want students to be able to construct legitimate arguments for and against any epistemology about which they have read.

You are likely to up your grades because of group discussion. It is the most effective study technique that will improve your academics success drastically and up your grades.

You will never fail an assignment because you consulted with you group members and you helped each other and guide each other in putting it together. Because of group discussion I knew how to reference my assignment without having to look it up. The group offered guidance.

You will not fail an exam or a test if you are committed to group discussions. Content that you discuss comes from the syllabus and likely to be part of the test or exam.
