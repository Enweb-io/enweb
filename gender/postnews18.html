GENDER VIOLENCE

Dawn of violent fathers

1. Introduction The internet and Social media can expose young daughters to risks, including inappropriate content sharing and accesss to unwantd offensive media. Fathers can play a vital role in guiding their daughters safely without resorting to violence.

2. Open Communication Fathers should foster an open and non-judgmental environment where daughters feel comfortable discussing their social media activity and challenges.

3. Educate About Consequences Explain the potential social, emotional, and legal consequences of sharing inappropriate content. Help daughters understand how posts can affect future opportunities.

4. Set Clear Boundaries Establish guidelines for online behavior collaboratively. Boundaries should be fair, realistic, and explained clearly to ensure understanding.

5. Encourage Critical Thinking Teach daughters to think critically before posting: “Would I be comfortable if anyone saw this?” This develops self-awareness and responsible decision-making.

6. Positive Role Modeling Fathers and family members should model respectful and responsible online behavior, demonstrating how to use social media safely.

7. Use Technology Wisely Teach daughters about privacy settings, reporting harmful content, and digital footprint management. Tools should empower rather than control.

8. Encourage Hobbies and Offline Life Engage daughters in extracurricular activities and offline socialization. Balanced lifestyles reduce over-dependence on online validation.

9. Address Emotional Needs Inappropriate posting may reflect emotional needs or peer pressure. Fathers should listen, validate feelings, and guide daughters toward healthier coping strategies.

10. Conclusion Guiding daughters on social media safely requires patience, communication, education, and example-setting. Constructive guidance strengthens trust and equips young women to make responsible online choices without fear of punishment.
